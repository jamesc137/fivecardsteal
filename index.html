The Goal

Objective

Build the best 5-card poker hand by the end of the game.
Use strategy, memory, and timing to draw, steal, or swap cards while keeping your hand secret and in order. The twist? You're allowed to steal cards, and they're allowed to steal them back… eventually.

Before You Begin

Setup

  1. 1 Shuffle the deck and deal 5 cards face down to each player.
  2. 2 Cards should be placed left to right in a straight line, face down in front of each player.
  3. 3 Each player may look at their cards privately and rearrange them once before the game begins.
  4. 4 Once the first player starts, card positions may not change.
  5. 5 Place the remaining cards in the center of the table as the draw pile.
  6. 6 Leave space next to the draw pile for the discard row, a line of up to five face-up cards.
On Your Turn

Gameplay

Players take turns in clockwise order. On your turn, choose one of the following actions:

Draw from the Deck

  • Draw the top card from the draw pile.
  • Use it: Replace one of your 5 cards. Place the replaced card face up in the discard row.
  • Discard it: Place the drawn card face up in the discard row without changing your hand.

Steal from a Player

  • Choose any player and point to a card by position (e.g., "3rd from the left").
  • Take that card and give them one of your own in return.
  • Both cards stay face down in the same position they were taken from.
  • You may not steal back until after one full round has passed.

Take from Discard

  • Available from the second player's turn onward.
  • Take the most recently discarded card (top of the row).
  • Replace any card in your hand with it, and remove the replaced card from the game entirely.
Last Round

Game End

  • The game ends when the fifth card is added to the discard row.
  • The player who places or causes the fifth discard does not get another turn.
  • All remaining players take one final turn.
  • All players reveal their hands. The winner is the player with the highest-ranking poker hand.
  • In the event of a tie, use standard poker tiebreakers (e.g., high card, suit if needed).
Know Before You Play

Special Rules

Card Order

  • After setup, card positions are locked.
  • Replacements and steals must be placed back in the same slot.

Steal-Back Rule

  • A card cannot be stolen back until a full table rotation has passed.

Forgetting a Card

  • In standard mode, you may look at your cards at any time to remember them.

Win More Often

Strategy Tips

01

Don't rush to end the game, or maybe do. Ending the game blocks your own final turn, but forces everyone else into one last decision under pressure.

02

Use stealing tactically: to bluff, to remember what a position holds, or to disrupt an opponent who looks dangerously close to a strong hand.

03

Count the discard row. Everyone can see the end coming when four cards are down. How you play that last turn matters.
